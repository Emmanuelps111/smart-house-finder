Tenant Safety Advice

Protect yourself from rental scams in Tanzania — read this before sending any money.

Never send M-Pesa or Tigo Pesa before an in-person inspection

No genuine landlord will demand a deposit, "reservation fee" or booking payment before you have physically visited the property and met them face to face. If anyone asks you to send mobile money to hold a room — walk away, it is a scam.

Always inspect in person

Visit the property during daylight hours. Verify the address matches the map pin and that the room shown in the photos is the actual room being offered.

Verify the landlord

Ask to see a national ID and proof of ownership or a signed authorisation letter. Confirm the phone number on the listing matches the person you meet.

Sign a written agreement

Only pay rent or a deposit after both parties have signed a written tenancy contract in Kiswahili or English, stating the amount, duration and refund terms.

Keep proof of every payment

Request an M-Pesa/Tigo Pesa reference number and a signed receipt for every payment. Never pay in untraceable cash without a witness.

Report suspicious listings

If a listing looks too good to be true or the "landlord" pressures you to pay quickly, report it to us at support@smarthousefinder.co.tz so we can remove it and warn other renters.
